Key Terms GlossaryHover any highlighted term above for a quick definition
YTD (Year-to-Date)
Performance measured from January 1st of the current year to today. "BDO +22.3% YTD" means BDO's stock price has risen 22.3% since the start of 2026.
P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings)
How many pesos investors pay for every ₱1 of a company's annual earnings. A P/E of 10x means the stock costs 10× its yearly profit. Lower P/E can suggest undervaluation.
Market Capitalization
Total market value of a company — its share price multiplied by the total number of shares. Large Cap companies on the PSE are generally above ₱100 billion in market cap.
Dividend Yield
Annual cash dividends paid per share, divided by the share price. A yield of 4% means you receive ₱4 in dividends for every ₱100 invested. Higher yields attract income investors.
Beta
Measures a stock or sector's sensitivity to PSEi movements. Beta of 1.0 = moves with the index. Beta above 1.0 = more volatile than the market. Beta below 1.0 = more stable.
Volume (Trading Volume)
Number of shares traded in a given period. High volume on an up day confirms buying interest. High volume on a down day confirms selling pressure. Low volume moves are less reliable.
